Please Leave Your Message After the Tone
by Jhonny Lemus
Dear Love
Where do I begin
Writing this poem to you
Attempting to save
What I've become a slave to
Something long gone
But so close to my heart
A big bang, a broken union
So far, so close, so gone
And it struck me, as it strikes me every time
To call you, to call for you
When I was there a'pondering
An epiphany when I didn't need one
And to whom do I owe the pleasure to?
Because my body can't handle the truth
Payphone,
You're all alone
I'm sorry
I don't have the spare change to run through your area codes
To call and hear her voice
When all I hear are the ghosts of an empty wood
Stranded I've become
In crowds so full of empty people
Wondering if I'm ready to meet you
I have found this place they only write about in my fiction books
Where digits run away from this phone in endless patterns
Like a stream along a river
I'm just drifting by
With my back against the water
My head to the sky.
And…
how beautiful the goodbyes
When neither of us could hang up
I guess that's why I'm always leaving you a message
and you never pick up
Her eyes haunt my thoughts in the day
Leaving my mind to wander alone
In this dark place
My mind dismantled by a perpetual crisis
Driven to a sickly madness that smells of nostalgia
A theatrical drama is what im dialing for
It's what I'm going to die for
A Fantasia Number 1, two eyes one gun
She glides above the digits
Like a reaper that's not a cropper
Moving away from the cellular walls
Through the speakers of the caller.
My restless dream
Like my "Silent Hill"
I cannot escape this hyperplane
Between us…
Because maybe I like to suffer and its over
When I'm thinking of us…
Us….
It's like a movie when she turns around to face me
Black hair, black eyes
Hair flying in the wind like a free and radiant light
I'm supposed to smile like everything is okay
That's the part I play when I'm on screen
Where behind the call-curtain
I'm going to cry and call myself a man
When I never had a plan to handle way the numbers fly through my brain
I'm going insane because I don't sleep
When it's always just a dream
A restless thing that keeps me up
On nights which never end
A perpetual love that never sleeps
A smile that runs cheek to cheek
In cheap photos I can't break apart
I pore over all the details of our bond
As tears pour down onto photopaper
In love notes that wrenches my heart
The feeling in my gut that calls me to call you
Like Eyre to Rochester.
How foretelling when we spoke of Gatsby
And now all I think about is her.
As stars were being sprinkled from her hands
To speckle this space
As the swans bow their heads in unison
To acknowledge her grace
And even when the water calms to take a listen
To the way her body sways in smooth motion
Like a ballet dancer on leaping through air
Like a violin bow to violin
Remind me of hidden dances
We shared in gardens, rooms and rain
And in elevators that took us so high
Our hands a single of immovable
We thought we'd never stop…
But somehow we did…
Payphone
I'm sending this message at the tone
Because I'm more confused than truly gone
Beating myself as I rip the pages of these yellow magazines
Sticking together the way things used to be
But I can't find the right way to put together
The cipher that spells out her name
Nothing works, and I'm running out of time
But it will never be the same
I wrote to you in fountain pen
I never had the chance to place it in your hands
To displace stardust between the letters
Where swans swim by
Atop white letterpaper
To the ends of worn out sweaters
To deliver what I cannot say
As I drown in black ink, trying to find an escape
So I must fly away on a paper plane
Built from things I don't know how to say
Ripping myself apart as I rip apart the pages
Of the things you never knew.
I cannot rearrange the characters of this play
That won't stop playing the same plot I cannot change
I'm losing control of the stage
As it disintegrates into madness
I descent into darkness
That's really just a mask for a suffocating sadness
As I float on ink that was once
The blood of my veins
When you are the sole occupation
Of my thoughts
Where I'm not safe
But I'm not yet dead…
Why…
Why…(I whisper to the ink that has consumed me)
Her eyes glow and glow in this place between life and death they only speak about
In my "fiction" books
Watching me, but somehow she finally came…
Soft…
Soft are her eyes
Because they represent how they touched my soul
And between the pages she's so…
Real…
And Alive…
Reaching for my fingertips through the guise
Of my meaningless words
That can't cross worlds but I try so hard to find the way
The way to what?
But…
through the pages she touches my soul
Her touch surpasses the imperfect skin
That soothe the tensest fibers of my body
Correcting the imperfections
Entering every intersection of my whole being.
She unravels me through the far stretches
Of rushed penstrokes
Asking me me more than questions
Is she watching me from afar
Like a lighthouse daydreaming
As it watches stones sink from its cliffs
Watching ships sink from her Earth
Besting my nature and
Capsizing all of existence?
She could stop time itself
In what she could make a single kiss
With those lovely eyes,
There she stays and watches
As my lips quiver before tears
Because I'm just imagining this
And im holding it all in
Trying to steer the uncharted course
But it's just too much.
The tension of broken cords
Held on for so long just to snap
Anchoring me to bottomless fantasies
And to lost connections
Somewhere halfway through
Your voice vanished to the swans
So I missed your laughter
And instead of being grateful
It just echoes in this empty space
As a sweet giggle I cannot cherish anymore.
Somehow I have become the house and the walls
Trapping the bounds of my closure,
And the closure of our relationship.
So my shadow meets my spirit like an old friend
And tells me its over
Because it becomes ever the more painful
Each time I hear it
Love you can't explain to me
Why I'm sitting in my car
With a gun in my hand
And your voice from afar
Before I start the new Big Bang
Watching the moon and sun cycle like a washing machine
To clean out the good and the bad
Like a slow working disease
Because I'm suffering a moral dilemma.
You come and then you go
Just like the swans that stare at me
With their daring purity
Judging me with their unquestionable clarity
Asking me to stop watching those
Starry skies, those starry eyes of your ghost
Your phantom, calling a home
In my vessel, my heart
And what did you expect me to do
To carry you on my shoulders
As my life passes? As I get older?
When I'm living on the edge? The edge of my
desk?
When you've assigned me this task
To make sense of nonsense?
And it kills me that I can't?
You're trying to call home, when I want you to leave
Because I am the walls too
And what is the thing your trying to show me
What is this pain you paid in full
When I cannot spare another memory
Of you, for you?
But I'm alone wondering what I am
Laying in the gaze of what would be my demise
Where I shutter at the beautiful song
That are the irises of her eyes
That explode with glittered firework
Laying in the comfort and vulnerability
Of a love now lost
And now you want me to see her
When she's all I see already (it's the dream)
Now I break away from invisible hands
That have tethered my blood to the car crash about to happen
Because I too became invisible
In a land which doesn't exist to man
In a state you managed to fund me in
Where bullets change men but can't kill them.
BANG,
CRASH.
My greatest fear is to fail you
And my greatest failure is my fear of such
Now your hands have left the wheel
Leaving me to burn in the ship
That had already sunk
A grand man-of-war
That's sailed across the divine thunderstorm
To carry the bad in me to a sea of ink
And lift the good in me to life
Because I've been saying goodbye to myself too many times
Because my anger feeds a hopeless rage
That tries to drive me insane
My blood, boiling, as your lips
Slip the words that aren't lies
But avoid the truth

That aren't meant to break
Only there to take it in because I can't
Only there to take it and keep it in
Because that's all they can do
And that hurts me most
More than I ever knew
But now I understand
Because the swans explained it to me

So I ask how many times did we play like children
To suffer as adults?
The flavors of the world so bland
When the colors of your companionship are now gone
And the distance has only grown a new pain
Of regret and wishful thinking
Because your being is somehow there in the life I breath in
Haunting me like a vampires ghoul
Staring into my eyes around every streetcorner
Haunting me in your phantom clothes and
Dressed up as the things that have remained the dream
Formless and yet so invigorating
Haunting my memories
Because everything speaks your name
But could I ever give up…
Like some apocalypse
It has left this nameless void in me
And I wonder everyday if you feel the same
I wonder because I have one life to live
And I've grown attached to you beyond understanding
And maybe it's something I used to write poems about
About this thing called love
And maybe I never really understood it until now
But I'm leaving you a message now
As sweet as strawberries come
Only the ones you gave me were the sweetest
Sometimes I'd wait and write a poem
At your special place, waiting to read them to you because maybe you'd show up again
Hoping you'd come randomly but you never did
And every night I'd watch from those hidden rooftops
Above the hush of those sleeping streets
Where the streetlamps separate me from the living
And only I could see your large resting eyes on the skyline
The buildings caught into your dark silhouette
The beauty of night coalesced into you
Your eyes folding like sparkling stars coming out of rotation
As they blink like a widows web
So I could read my poems to you
Im heartfelt ovation
Capturing all of the city's nightlife
In a pair of dark blinking jewels

To fall asleep
I would come back to life
Because then…
I became a whisper,
threaded through the embers of your dreams
Your ghost comes as innocent swans to

As meanings in dreams

An artifact that binded us forever
And maybe between the half truths and half lies
We missed the mark because we lived what felt like a lifetime of romance
But in all of the chaos I choose you
And I never want to look back
I choose you in these lonely hours
Where the dream lives forever
Where I've heard myself cry like a child.
But I stand where you left
And if you can meet me there
Where we once went our separate ways
We can still fix what we began
As we throw doves to lofty winds
The swans told me it must be this way…
And no other words can describe it as so -
The restless dreams which haunt me still.
But you never pick up
You never will…
The pianos are against the jaded walls
Waiting and waiting to be played,
Waiting for life to hit them because they don't know how beautiful they sound

And play the most beautiful sounds to grow the fractured fields of hope
